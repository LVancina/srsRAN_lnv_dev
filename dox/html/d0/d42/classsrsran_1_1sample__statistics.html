Remarks
The function returns NAN when the sample consists of less than three observations.
Note
The skewness provides a rough indication of the symmetry of the distribution of the sample around its mean. More specifically, for unimodal distributions, a negative-valued skewness means that the left tail of the distribution is longer than the right one and most of the mass is concentrated to the right of the mean. In other words, the median is higher than the mean. A positive skewness means the opposite. Symmetric distributions have null skewness. However, the opposite is not always true as, e.g., a long left tail and a fat right tail may compensate each other and give skewness equal to zero.
