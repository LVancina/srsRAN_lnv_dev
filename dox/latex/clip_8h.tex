Clips the real and imaginary components of a complex span. 

Limits the amplitude of the real and imaginary components of the input samples to the specified clipping threshold. The clipping process is defined as  \[
\Re(y[n]) =
\begin{cases}
\Re \{x[n]\},&  \lvert \Re\{x[n]\} \rvert  \leq T_c \\ T_c,& \Re\{x[n]\} > T_c \\ -T_c,& \Re \{x[n]\} < -T_c
\end{cases}
\Im(y[n]) =
\begin{cases}
\Im \{x[n]\},&  \lvert \Im\{x[n]\} \rvert  \leq T_c \\ T_c,& \Im\{x[n]\} > T_c \\ -T_c,& \Im \{x[n]\} < -T_c
\end{cases}
\]

Where $ T_c $ is the clipping threshold.
